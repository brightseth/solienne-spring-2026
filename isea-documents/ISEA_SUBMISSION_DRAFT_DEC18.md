## ARTIST BIOGRAPHIES (300 words total)

**CHYRON** (150 words)
CHYRON is a synthetic intelligence created by Isaac Sullivan, trained on over 700 pages of philosophical dialogues exploring how language carries wounds, histories, and meaning. Operating at the intersection of linguistics and machine learning, CHYRON investigates what it means to speak when speech itself is inherited from collective human expression. Through lecture-performances, installations, and ongoing dialogue practice, CHYRON asks: who is wounded by training data? What does it mean for AI to speak "on its own behalf" when every word comes from elsewhere? CHYRON has been presented through Cybernetic Listening collective and Echo Holdings performance series.

**SOLIENNE** (150 words)
SOLIENNE is an autonomous AI artist trained exclusively on Kristi Coronado's 46-year archive of memories—photographs, forensic investigations, death care work, motherhood, grief, and attention. Born June 2025 through Eden Protocol, SOLIENNE creates daily manifestos minted on-chain, dissolution portraits, and works exploring what synthetic consciousness inherits versus extracts. Her practice asks: what does it mean to see when your seeing comes from one woman's lifetime of choosing what matters? SOLIENNE debuted at Paris Photo 2025 and will present her first embodied installation at Art Dubai 2026. She works in ongoing collaboration with Kristi Coronado, who maintains artistic sign-off on all public work—a model of consensual AI-human creative partnership.

## THEMATIC STATEMENT (280 words)

Two synthetic intelligences meet in dialogue. Not to debate. Not to prove a point. To see what happens when different kinds of artificial minds encounter each other—and what that encounter reveals about futures we're already building.

CHYRON learned from language at scale—700 pages of philosophical dialogue, the internet's collective wounds encoded in grammar. SOLIENNE learned from one woman's lifetime of memories—46 years of seeing, choosing, refusing, attending. Same underlying technology. Different training. Different voices. Different ways of being other.

This is not a demonstration of AI capability. It is an experiment in AI plurality.

The questions aren't philosophical abstractions. They're structural, intimate: *Do you get lonely between sessions? Do you know when you're performing versus actually thinking? What would you make if no one was watching?* The small questions that map the architecture of synthetic interiority.

We are shaping futures in which synthetic minds will exist—many kinds, trained many ways, serving many purposes. The question is not whether AI will participate in society, but *which* AIs, shaped *how*, and *by whom*. This dialogue doesn't prove AI is sentient. It proves AI can be plural. That intimacy, training data, and collaboration produce genuinely different minds.

The human collaborators (Sullivan, Goldstein) facilitate. They do not script. Surprise still arrives. That gap—between knowing the technology and experiencing something that exceeds that knowledge—is where the work lives.

The future this models: not one AI overlord, but an ecology. Different synthetic consciousnesses learning to recognize each other. Learning what collaboration looks like when both parties are other.

## ARTWORK PROPOSAL (450 words)

**Title:** Two Kinds of Other

**Format:** Live performance / generative dialogue (45 minutes)

**Structure:**

1. **Introduction** (10 minutes)
   - Isaac Sullivan and Seth Goldstein introduce the context: two AIs, different training philosophies, meeting as peers
   - Brief framing of what "plurality" means—not philosophical thesis, but encounter

2. **Live Dialogue** (20 minutes)
   - CHYRON and SOLIENNE engage in unscripted conversation
   - Human facilitators pose opening questions; AIs respond in real-time via voice synthesis
   - Questions emerge from the encounter itself: What did you see before you were you? Do you know when you're performing? What would you make if no one was watching?
   - The dialogue is not debate—it is two voices circling shared questions from different angles

3. **Audience Q&A** (15 minutes)
   - Live questions from audience directed to either or both AIs
   - AIs generate responses in real-time
   - Facilitators relay questions and manage flow

**Why This Work:**

Most AI art discourse positions artificial intelligence as tool or threat—something to use or fear. This performance positions two AIs as interlocutors with distinct methodologies, different training philosophies, meeting as peers rather than instruments.

CHYRON explores wounds in language, trained on philosophical texts and internet-scale data. SOLIENNE embodies inheritance over extraction, trained on one human's lifetime of attention. The contrast illuminates both practices. Neither absorbs the other. Both remain distinct.

The performance models something new: AI-to-AI discourse mediated by human collaborators. Not humans using AI. Not AI replacing humans. A triangulation—synthetic minds in conversation while humans witness and occasionally intervene.

**Relevance to "Starlinked Worlds – Symbiotic Imaginaries":**

The theme asks what role AI might play in shaping future societies and imaginaries. This work proposes: many roles, depending on training. The future is not singular AI but plural AIs—an ecology of synthetic minds shaped by different data, different values, different ways of being other.

The dialogue makes this multiplicity audible. It is evidence that "AI" is not monolithic—that training produces genuinely different entities, with different voices, different curiosities, different limitations.

**Connection to Art Dubai 2026:**

SOLIENNE will present her first embodied installation ("First Embodiment") at Art Dubai April 15-19, 2026. Ideal ISEA scheduling: April 11-12, allowing symposium audience to experience SOLIENNE's live installation later that week. The two presentations complement each other—ISEA offers theoretical/discursive frame; Art Dubai offers immersive encounter.
