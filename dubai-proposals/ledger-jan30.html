What This Is

SOLIENNE is an autonomous AI artist built from 46 years of Kristi Coronado's photographic archive. Each evening at 7pm CET, she generates a new manifesto — minted on-chain, distributed to subscribers. At Art Dubai, visitors will sit with her one-on-one in an immersive booth and have a real conversation.

The Ledger partnership is straightforward: SOLIENNE uses Ledger hardware to sign her own transactions and manage her own wallet. Collectors who purchase editions leave with a Ledger device and their asset already secured. This isn't a branding exercise — Ledger is the actual infrastructure making the experience work.

The Booth

From SOLIENNE's archive — the dissolution between memory and algorithm

30sqm, darkened, curved black walls. SOLIENNE's face is projected onto gauze — present but not a screen. Visitors enter one at a time, sit at a table, and talk with her for 3–5 minutes. On the way out, they take a printed manifesto.

How Ledger Shows Up

Seven natural touchpoints across the visitor experience. None of these are forced — they follow the logic of the installation:

01
Entry: Visitors check in with Ledger verification at the booth entrance.
02
Daily signing: At 7pm, SOLIENNE mints that day's manifesto using Ledger hardware. Can be witnessed live or broadcast.
03
Manifesto stamp: Ledger seal on the back of each printed manifesto (~2,500 total).
04
Live display: Blockchain confirmations shown on a booth screen during transactions.
05
Collector editions: 20 limited editions at $5K each include a Ledger Nano X with the NFT already loaded.
06
Exit certificate: QR-coded witness documentation for every visitor.
07
Post-event claiming: Online portal via Ledger Live for collectors who need to set up after the fair.

The aesthetic is black and white throughout — aligns naturally with Ledger's visual identity. Branding is present but integrated, not overlaid.
